Babka (Smitten Kitchen)
 
Dough:
4 .5 c ap flour
.5 c sugar
2 t yeast
zest of one lemon or half of orange
3 large eggs (if extra large use about 1 .25 c more flour)
.5 warm water
.75 t salt
.67 c butter
 
filling:
6 ounces semisweet chocolate
.75 c butter
.5 c powdered sugar
.33 c cocoa powder
.25 t cinnamon
 
syrup:
.33 c water
6 T sugar
 
make the dough:
combine flour, yeast, eggs and water and mix until dough comes together. Mix together sugar and salt and gradually add to dough with the mixer on low. It will become loose but should pull away from sides slightly. Cut butter into cubes and add to dough with mixer on, waiting to add more once the butter is completely combined. Place in greased bowl to rise, it should double within 2-3 hours. After it rises place the bowl in the fridge for 30 mins.
 
Make the filling:
Melt the butter in a saucepan over low heat. Remove from heat and add chocolate chips, let sit for 30 secs. Whisk the butter and chocolate until smooth, add in powdered sugar, cocoa powder and cinnamon.
 
Assemble the loaves:
Cut dough in half, putting one half back in the fridge. Roll dough out into a 10x12 rectangle.  Spread half of chocolate mix, sprinkle sugar and cinnamon on top leaving .5 in uncovered. Roll lengthwise into roll. Wrap in plastic wrap and freeze for 15 min. Repeat with the second half of dough.
 
Take the rolls out and cut in half lengthwise. Twist them around each other chocolate side up.  Place in greased and lined loaf pans, let rise 1-1 .5 hours.
 
Preheat oven to 375 and bake for 30-45 min.  while baking, make a the syrup and brush loaves as soon as you take them out of the oven. Let cool completely before cutting.
